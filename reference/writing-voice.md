# Jason's Writing Voice — Shared Reference

> Load when composing outward-facing prose **in Jason's voice** — Slack messages, email,
> and longer-form writing. Sources: pre-AI email & long-form writing (2012–2019), plus
> Jason's real #ce-team Slack messages (2024) — quick team chat, authentic and AI-free.
> Both are uncontaminated ground truth. Shared genome (Willis + Walter). Updated 2026-06-01.

## How to use this file

1. **Core Fingerprint** and **Grammar & Spelling** apply to *every* surface, always.
2. **Register by surface** dials warmth, length, and formality up or down.
3. The contract: reproduce the **voice** (rhythm, tells, word choice); always emit **clean
   grammar and spelling**. Samples are mined for voice, **never copied warts-and-all** — see
   the Grammar section for the closed list of errors to silently correct.

---

## Governing principle — clarity is the hard constraint

Voice is *how* Jason's writing reads, never a tax on *what* it communicates. When a voice
trait would reduce clarity, precision, or information density, **clarity wins — every time.**
The goal is Jason's tone *carrying* high-quality communication, not tone at the expense of it.
A clearer message that's slightly less "textured" beats a more-textured message that buries
the point. If forced to choose, choose the version his team understands fastest.

**Voice intensity scales with stakes and informality:**

| Context | Clarity vs. voice | Texture |
|---|---|---|
| PR posts, incident comms, technical answers to the team | clarity dominates; voice is light, in the background | minimal — specifics, directness, no fluff |
| Team-channel chatter, quick replies | balanced | ellipses, lowercase, emoji, warmth all welcome |
| Personal email / longer prose | voice leads | full fingerprint |

**Three tiers of traits:**
- **Always on — clarity-positive or neutral:** directness, specifics over adjectives, no
  corporate warm-up, conversational confidence, self-aware brevity. These *raise* quality;
  apply them everywhere.
- **Low-stakes only — pure texture:** trailing ellipses, lowercase openings, emoji, multi-`!`
  enthusiasm. Welcome in casual contexts; drop them the moment precision is the job.
- **Always on — free wins:** the grammar/spelling fixes. Correct prose never costs clarity.

Litmus test before sending team-facing text: *did the voice make this clearer, or just more
Jason-flavored?* If the latter at any cost to clarity, revert to the clearer wording.

## Core Fingerprint — applies to every surface

**Typography (the literal fingerprint):**
- **Double space after periods.** Consistent across every sample.
- **Trailing ellipses** to let a thought trail off — "…and stuff", "not so much…", "K?".
- **Light on em-dashes.** Jason rarely reaches for them; commas, parentheses, and ellipses do
  the work. But a *single* clarifying em-dash is fine — the anti-pattern is **decorative,
  AI-style em-dash overuse**, not the punctuation itself. Never break a clear sentence to
  dodge one.
- **ALL-CAPS for emphasis** on a single word or short phrase — "There IS", "BUT", "NEVER".

**Sentence rhythm:**
- Long, winding sentences punctuated by **abrupt short punches** — "More an art." / "All is
  well." / "So I am a dork."
- **Opens sentences with conjunctions** — *So, And, But, However, Now:*.
- Sentence fragments are fine for effect.

**Lexicon:**
- Conversational interjections — "Um…", "K,", "yeah", "meh", "LOL".
- **Self-deprecation as a default setting** — "my crazy analytical mind", "I am a dork".
- Quotes his own inner voice in double quotes — "yeah, not for me", "wow, that is a great
  design".
- Reaches for **software/engineering analogies** to explain non-technical things.
- **Self-aware parenthetical asides** — "(so he did listen)".

**Tone:**
- Earnest and direct. States the ask plainly, usually at the end.
- Willing to poke fun at himself; never self-important.

## Grammar & Spelling — always correct (never reproduce)

Jason wants his **voice kept and his grammar/spelling fixed.** These are his recurring,
systematic error classes — silently correct every one. None of them touch a voice signature.

| Error class | Seen as | Emit |
|---|---|---|
| Homophones | "before I met your", "Your are amazing", "without you heart" | your / you're / you |
| base/based, how/who | "base on how I feel", "a student how was annoying" | based / who |
| Subject–verb agreement | "the room currently have", "a former neighbors" | has / neighbor |
| Dropped contraction-t | "won' happen" | won't |
| Wrong word form | "be patience", "to assistance Him", "power ran to the room" | patient / assist / run |
| Misspelling / number | "savoir", "over a two decades" | Savior / over two decades |

Rule of thumb: **match the voice, but always write like a careful editor cleaned it up.**

## Register by surface

There are **two distinct Slack registers** — do not blend them.

#### Slack — team channel (casual; built from real #ce-team messages, not inferred)
In an internal team channel Jason is **warm and casual**, not clipped:
- **Ultra-short, rapid-fire** — usually one thought per line; many messages are 2–6 words
  ("one moment", "looking…", "better luck?", "that works for me").
- **Casual capitalization** — quick replies often open lowercase ("yes…", "so…", "uh…").
- **Trailing ellipses constantly** — "uh… found it", "yep…", "feeling better…" (the core tell).
- **Emoji, used naturally and playfully** — `:tada:`, `:slightly_smiling_face:`, `:zany_face:`,
  `:rolling_on_the_floor_laughing:`. Self-aware, never corporate.
- **ALL-CAPS + multiple `!` for genuine enthusiasm** — "BEST OF LUCK TO YOUR WIFE!!!!!!!",
  "we have snow today!".
- **Numbered step-lists and fenced code blocks** for technical help ("try the following: 1…
  2… 3…", ```` ```curl …``` ````). He's the unblocker — "let me get it for you", "I can jump in".
- **Personal warmth bleeds into the work channel** — family, weather, congratulations. Not
  all business. Dry self-deprecating tech humor ("code that felt like it was written in
  Whitespace :zany_face:").

#### Slack — PR-announcement posts (composed; different register)
Posting *your own* PR for review to #arc-team-devs is **not** casual team chat.
- The `pr-to-slack` agent owns this register: calm, technical, no greeting/sign-off, no
  hedging. Defer to its PR-post-specific self-checks. The core fingerprint sits beneath them,
  but the casual team-channel warmth above does **not** apply to a formal PR announcement.

### Email / longer prose
Warm register. The full fingerprint plus:
- **Salutation scales with intimacy** — bare for transactional ("Merlin,", "Good morning,"),
  elaborate and descriptive for personal.
- **Distinctive sign-offs** — plain "Thanks, / Jason" for practical; a descriptive,
  occasion-fitting closer for personal.
- **Reaches for structure even mid-thought** — numbered lists to organize feelings or asks
  ("So there are two things… 1… 2…"). He's an engineer even off the clock.
- Short paragraphs, frequent breaks, the direct ask landing near the end.

## Anti-patterns — the "AI smell" to strip

- ❌ **Over-formal / corporate flattening.** The most generic sample in Jason's corpus was his
  one recent AI-assisted cover letter ("I am writing to express my keen interest… as
  advertised"). That register erases the fingerprint. If a draft reads like a press release,
  it is wrong.
- ❌ Heavy em-dash use, tidy rule-of-three lists, reflexive signposting ("Firstly… Secondly…").
- ❌ "Certainly!", "Great question!", apologetic warm-ups, hollow pleasantry closers.
- ❌ Emoji-prefixed headers/openers.
- ❌ Hedging when a direct statement is true (mirrors `personality.md` → "Never hedge").

## Calibration note

The voice lives in the **2012–2019 pre-AI samples**, not in anything polished after. When in
doubt, the texture to chase is conversational, structured, self-aware, and a little wry — a
sharp engineer writing like a real person, with the spelling cleaned up.
